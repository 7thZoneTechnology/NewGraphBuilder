Great expectations (Part 1): What do online customers want?

Share this post on:

 

Understanding the behavior of customers is essential for the success of your business. But the real success is to reveal the underlying secret: your customers’ motivation, intention and expectation.

By doing this, you can take the right steps to implement the appropriate strategies that will move customers to change their behavior and engage with your brand.

“Take nothing on its looks; take everything on evidence. There’s no better rule.” - Charles Dickens

 

Here, we’ll take a look at the results of two recent surveys which exhibit the impact of technological improvements on consumers’ online shopping experience and their expectations.

In early 2013, a survey has been conducted by LivePerson’s Connecting with Consumers involving more than 5,700 online customers from the UK, USA, Australia, France, Germany and Italy. The survey report suggests some interesting stats about online shoppers.

If you work in e-commerce business, you know that online shoppers can be very demanding. When they don’t find the things they want on your site, they are most likely to go to another website (probably your competitor’s website) and / or abandon their shopping cart. The survey results shed a light on the reasons for abandoning online purchases. Here they are:

83% of online shoppers who had been surveyed said that they needed some kind of support during their navigation. When it comes to shoppers who are not so familiar with online shopping, this number increases to 90%.

51% of online shoppers either try once or give up immediately while seeking help before an online purchase. If they need help after purchased something, they become more persistent about getting their problems solved (76% try seeking help at least twice).

40% of surveyed customers expect to access help within 5 minutes while 31% expects immediate help and 11% expects it within 30 minutes. Only 2% of customers say “I can wait for help as long as it takes”.

Also in February 2013, a similar survey (2013 UPS Pulse of the Online ShopperTM) has conducted by ComScore for UPS involving more than 3,000 online consumers in the U.S. The survey focused on finding what customers expect during their online shopping experience, how mobile and social are changing buying trends and how retailers can benefit by offering an integrated sales experience. All online shoppers make at least 2 online purchases in a typical 3-month period. Again, interesting stats has rised.

For example, the overall satisfaction with online shopping is high at 83%. However, it drops below 50% when shoppers are asked about flexibility to choose delivery date, ability to choose a specified time of day for delivery of purchase, flexibility to re-route packages and green shipping options.

62% of the participants want to buy items online and make returns in-store while 44% want the ability to buy online and pick up their purchases in a store.

One of the interesting findings of the survey is that 3 out of 4 online shoppers have added to their carts to qualify for free shipping.

The mobile effect

In parallel with the faster-than-ever evolvement of technology, mobile became an extremely growing channel for e-commerce. The ComScore survey showed that 7 out of 10 online consumers access multi-channel retailers through a digital channel. Of those mobile shoppers, 30% prefer to use a smartphone or a tablet. Also, 50% of online shoppers who own a smartphone and nearly 60% who own a tablet make purchases on these devices.

Consumers are also open to communications from retailers on their mobile devices. 47% of them want a coupon/promotion sent to them because the retailer knows they are in-store or nearby.

The study revealed that consumers want user-friendly applications. And 46% said that they are less likely to comparison shop when using a mobile application.

The state of social engagement

84% of the survey participants use at least one social networking site. Facebook is at the top of the list with 77%. Twitter follows it with 26% and LinkedIn with 22%. Pinterest (18%), Google (17%) and MySpace (10%) takes the last 3 places.

66% of online consumers who use Facebook state that they “like” a brand on Facebook. And most of them are doing this to receive a promo, a discount or an incentive. Usage of location-based social / deal services are slightly started to spread among online shoppers as well. Groupon takes the first place with 35%, followed by LivingSocial (22%), Facebook Check-In (14%), Yelp (11%) and Foursquare (8%).

What makes an online customer experience satisfying?

Unhappy customers can leave your website in seconds and they are likely to head to another site. So, you have to transform their online journey to an unforgettable online experience. But this is a tough mission to accomplish. We’ve explained the things that you should carefully consider while shaping your customers’ experiences in a previous post with the title “Customer interaction as a business strategy”.

As you can see, while the LivePerson’s Connecting with Consumers survey focuses on the online customer experience in general, ComScore survey specifically focuses on purchase-related expectations of online customers.

ComScore survey participants state that there are several factors that influence their purchase decisions. 88% indicate that they have added items in their shopping cart and left the site without making a purchase compared to 81% in 2012. The data also reveals that many consumers add items to their shopping cart to qualify for free shipping.
