2012-05-23

Zauber announces Social Media Analytics suite

Big news ! We are excited to announce the release of our Social Media Analytics suite of products: Flowics and Tribatics, the most recent and ambitious creations of our Lab and the result of months of hard work in the field of Big Data and AI.

Flowics is a platform to create real-time visualizations out of Twitter conversations. Flowics collects, filters and visually presents trends and indicators of the buzz around a specific show, event, or news story. Flowics allows producers to make their coverage even more engaging as it provides an actual visualization of that buzz.



Tribatics provides quality insights on social audiences and conversations. The platform processes large-scale social media data in real time, identifying behavioral and demographic patterns on Twitter followers and conversation topics, to be used for decision making, market research, brand monitoring and improving online communication strategies.



These two products are focusing initially on Twitter, but support for more social networks is coming soon.

We realized that traditional monitoring of social media has not been evolving enough, even though the market is asking for that. In particular, and unlike with other social networks, whatever happens on Twitter affects in real-time and is picked up by mainstream media channels which amplify it. Tools that allow for profound measurement and thorough analysis are essential in order to work with real-time media like Twitter, as opposed to traditional monitoring tools which just scrape the surface of the Web. Social media requires superior level of qualitative analysis, which is what Tribatics and Flowics provide.

So, get to know the audience of any relevant Twitter account (even of your competitors), discover the real audience and demographics behind any popular conversation, hashtag or topic on Twitter and easily create and publish the most powerful real-time visualizations of social media buzz. Start using Tribatics and Flowics today and be welcome to a new era in Social Media Analytics !

2011-12-07

Zauber raises USD 1 million to power its Lab

Hooray! We are excited to announce that Zauber has raised USD 1 million in our first venture capital financing round !

Led by AxVentures - Pymar Fund, also an investor at Cupoint (a spin off from our Lab), the new funding will enable our company to expand our boutique high-end software development services in our target markets of Silicon Valley and Brazil, as well as heavily grow our investment in R+D and new products development at our Lab.

This is a huge achievement for Zauber, which reinforces our vision of building a company that combines the offer of high-end software development services with the incubation of new products and ventures.

Here's a little bit of our story:

Almost five years ago, six friends and graduates from ITBA (Gabriel, Juan, Mariano, Martín, Andrés and Fernando) got together with the idea of starting a new venture: a software technology company with a strong focus on R+D and innovation.

It was February 2007. We literally started as a garage company, with an old dark basement at one of our partner’s parents’ home serving as our office, and a ping-pong table serving as our desks. Three of the founders started the operations, while three other founders kept their jobs for some months funding with their salaries the living of all the six. Four months after the startup, we already had our first clients and we were making some humble revenue that we used to hire our first two employees (Christian and Marcelo). Almost five years later, they remain still at the company with us, they are key members of our senior team and remind us every day that we are doing things the right way.

We have been able to bootstrap our business without the need of external funding until now. We grew our business and assembled a talented team of 37 people. We partnered with other startups, helping them to kick off their projects with our technology and know how. We have global clients in global markets. We had our ups and downs, but we never gave up, we were always loyal to our vision and values: to create a global software company which could attract the very best talent out there. We decided to raise money from VC to make innovation happen faster at our Lab, to expand our boutique business and launch new ventures. We were lucky to find the right investors, the ones who believed in this team and our vision.

Congratulations to all the Zauber team and special thanks to all our friends, colleagues and clients who helped us throughout these years to achieve this milestone. The best is yet to come !

2011-10-24

Twitter Analytics and Real-Time Monitoring for Argentinian Elections

Last Sunday, general elections took place in Argentina and once again Zauber was present with its technology to provide a real-time Twitter monitoring and analytics platform to track the behavior and buzz generated around this event.

Two of the most important online news media in Argentina chose Zauber to deploy an interactive real-time data visualization of Twitter and FB trends for the elections.

For La Nacion, we deployed a real-time dashboard tracking Twitter mentions for each candidate, measuring tweets per minute (buzz volume) and presenting that information with a powerful visualization, making the avatar of each candidate shine with each new tweet processed. We also measured the share of voice for each candidate. All statistic were available for the last hour, last day and last week, and were updated in real-time minute after minute.

For Infobae, we deployed another dashboard tracking Twitter mentions of each candidate in time and measuring share of voice, with a different visual representation. We added to this dashboard information about Twitter followers growth and Facebook Fans growth for each main candidate, being able to compare one candidate's metric with each other.

We are happy with these two projects that we deployed, and thankful to La Nación and Infobae, who trusted Zauber to build these innovative products for such a big event as the presidential elections. For several days, we processed hundreds of thousands of tweets referred to the elections and the candidates, delivering content to thousands of users who visited these products.

2011-07-11

Estadísticas y Monitoreo en Twitter para Elecciones Buenos Aires

(this post is written in Spanish, an English version will be released soon).

En el día de ayer Domingo 10 de Julio de 2011, tuvo lugar la primera vuelta de las elecciones a Jefe de Gobierno y Legisladores para la Ciudad Autónoma de Buenos Aires. Como ya hicimos en pasadas oportunidades para las elecciones presidenciales en Brasil en 2010, las elecciones legislativas en Argentina en 2009 o incluso este año para monitorear los conflictos en África y Medio Oriente para Al Jazeera, en esta oportunidad volvimos a poner nuestra tecnología y creatividad en juego para crear un nuevo experimento.

El viernes 8 de Julio por la noche publicamos online una visualización en tiempo real de menciones en Twitter de los principales candidatos a Jefe de Gobierno, con el fin de monitorear el nivel de "buzz" generado en torno a cada uno y cómo éste variaba minuto a minuto, junto con el cálculo de otras métricas de interés.

Visualización de menciones durante intervalo de 1 día:

Nuestro desarrollo fue destacado en TV y en diarios online.

Desde las 9 PM del Viernes 8 de Julio a las 9 AM del día Lunes 11, capturamos 122.000 tweets mencionando a los candidatos a jefe de gobierno más importantes, a través una conexión especial al API de Twitter denominada Firehose que nos garantizó el acceso al 100% de los tweets.

Mauricio Macri (@mauriciomacri) obtuvo su pico máximo de menciones en Twitter con 691 tweets/minuto a las 22:23 del día domingo, mientras que Daniel Filmus (@filmusdaniel) obtuvo su pico con 399 tweets/minuto a las 22:56 del mismo día. El resto de los candidatos no superó los 40 tweets/minuto, marca que registró Pino Solanas (@fernandosolanas) a las 21:48. Esto nos permite convalidar la existencia de una marcada polarización de la elección entre los dos candidatos principales, la cual queda demostrada por el análisis de un medio social como Twitter.

Trabajamos asimismo en el cálculo de otras métricas de interés, como son:

  • Ganancia de Followers por candidato
  • Distribución de menciones a cada candidato según clusters de audiencia (medida en función de followers de cada autor)
  • Distribución de menciones a cada candidato según clusters de autoridad (medida según el KloutScore de cada autor)
  • Nivel de "Buzz" generado por menciones a cada candidato y picos máximos obtenidos
  • Nivel de penetración ("share of voice") de cada candidato en el total de tweets referidos a ellos.

A continuación, una infografía completa con toda la información obtenida de la red social Twitter para los 122.000 tweets detectados: Nuestra visualización seguirá activa hasta la instancia de Ballotage.
