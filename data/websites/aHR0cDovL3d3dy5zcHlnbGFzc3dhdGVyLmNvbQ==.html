  • Energy

    Exploring and producing traditional and unconventional energy sources requires close monitoring of aquatic environments.  Whether monitoring natural or man-made hydrocarbon seeps in the ocean or recycling produced water for hydraulic fracturing, on-site monitoring can help safely produce energy more cost effectively.

  • Water Chemistry

    With automated chemical sensors, water samples don't need to be returned to a central lab. Deployed alone or paired with the Spyglass AutoSampler ("Porifera") system, the Spyglass Underwater Mass Spectrometer is an on-site analytical tool for volatile organic compounds (VOCs), semi volatiles, toxins, pollutants, biologicals, pharmaceuticals /PCPs (emerging contaminants) or metals in air or water. 

  • Mobile Sensor Platforms

    The Spyglass Solar-powered Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV) enables mobile sampling and analysis.  Results are transmitted directly to wireless networks on shore or via Spyglass relay station buoys.

  • Less than 1% and shrinking

    Ninety seven percent of Earth is covered in water. Less than 1% is suitable for drinking or commercial use.

  • Timely & Precise Data

    Water intensive industries require real-time, precise information on the biology and chemistry of aquatic environments.

  • Global Water Knowledge

    Spyglass is focused on enabling a global network of water sensors that employ state-of-the-art analytical methods previously restricted to central laboratories. These networks provide timely and precise data for the worldwide community of industry managers and researchers.
